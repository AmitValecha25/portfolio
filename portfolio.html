Publsh.ai Case Study

Project: An omni-channel PR distribution SaaS platform covering Print, Online, Radio, and Outdoor media.

Role: Associate Product Manager

Phase 1: Planning & Requirement Gathering

Authored the Product Requirement Document (PRD v1.0), defining all user/admin flows, payment integration with Stripe, notifications, and system exclusions to prevent scope creep. This created a single source of truth for all stakeholders.

Phase 2: Stakeholder Alignment & Governance

Ran weekly demos to collect structured feedback. Created an "Action Items Tracker" to clearly divide responsibilities between my team (tech changes) and the client (content inputs), improving trust and transparency.

Phase 3: Development & QA Management

Acted as the bridge between QA and developers. I verified staging builds, logged issues in Jira, and ensured GA4 and Hotjar analytics were correctly implemented for user behaviour tracking.

Key Process Improvements Introduced:

  • Phased Rollout: Launched core features first to meet the deadline, deferring content-heavy sections. This was critical for the on-time launch.
  • Templatised Campaign Briefs: Converted client requests into structured Google Docs, reducing back-and-forth communication.
  • Launch Readiness Checklist: Ensured no last-minute blockers by covering domains, payments, and legal pages proactively.

Outcome:

The platform launched on schedule despite client-side content delays. The new governance models (phased rollout, trackers) reduced the project cycle time by approximately 20% and were adopted as standard practice for future company projects.

Experttal Case Study

Project: A SaaS marketplace for employers, freelancers, and agencies with complex role-based permissions.

Role: Associate Product Manager

The Challenge

The project faced potential delays due to a large QA backlog, overlapping user permissions, and inconsistent workflows for job statuses.

My Solution & Contributions:

  • Created a Milestone-Driven Roadmap: Broke the project into 12 distinct stages from login to go-live, providing clarity and a clear path forward.
  • Introduced QA Prioritisation: Classified all issues as "Launch Critical," "Post-Launch," or "Not Required." This focused the development team on what truly mattered for a stable launch.
  • Standardised Job Lifecycle Flow: Defined clear stages for jobs (e.g., Shortlisted ‚Üí In Progress ‚Üí Closed) to resolve role confusion and create a consistent user experience.
  • Managed Stakeholder Expectations: Ran weekly reviews presenting the QA backlog and deferrals, which successfully reduced scope creep and late-stage surprises.

Outcome:

The platform launched with stable, end-to-end tested flows for registration, job posting, and messaging. The QA prioritisation strategy directly improved delivery efficiency by an estimated 15-20% and significantly dropped the post-launch defect rate.

Fun Ride Toys Case Study

Project: A Shopify-based eCommerce website for a toy company.

Role: Project Lead

The Challenge

The project was at risk of missing the crucial festive sales season due to significant client-side content delays and technical issues like poor homepage performance and broken cart UI.

My Solution & Contributions:

  • Implemented a Dummy Content Strategy: To unblock development, I negotiated a placeholder strategy for blogs and media pages, allowing the tech build to proceed without waiting on the client.
  • Created a Categorised Feedback Tracker: Grouped all issues into UI/UX, Content Gaps, and Performance. This gave both the development team and the client a clear, actionable list.
  • Introduced Phased QA Fixes: Separated "Must-Fix" launch-critical issues (e.g., broken checkout) from "Phase 2" optimisations (e.g., homepage speed). This ensured we focused on what was essential for launch.
  • Secured Incremental Sign-offs: Ran weekly client demos to get approval on completed sections, preventing last-minute disagreements and rework.

Outcome:

The site successfully launched before the festive season, preventing revenue loss. The phased approach ensured 90% of critical QA issues were resolved pre-launch. Post-launch optimisations led to a 30% improvement in homepage load time.
